📊 KA ESPORTS – PROFESSIONAL GLICKO-2 RANKING SYSTEM FOR MARIO KART 64

🧠 What is Glicko‑2 and why do we use it?

Glicko‑2 is a ranking system developed by Professor Mark Glickman as an improvement over the traditional Elo system. Unlike simple point systems, Glicko‑2 considers three factors for each player:

Rating (R)Represents the player's estimated skill100 – 3500
Rating Deviation (RD)Measures the uncertainty about the rating. More games = lower RD = more accurate rating30 – 350
Volatility (σ)Indicates performance consistency. Low values = stable results; high values = unpredictable player0.02 – 0.2

Advantages over traditional systems:

  • ✅ Quickly adjusts to the player's real skill level (thanks to RD)
  • ✅ Fairly penalizes those who play against very different skill levels
  • ✅ Detects and corrects performance inconsistencies (volatility)
  • ✅ All history matters, but recent matches have more weight

⚙️ System Components

• Rating (R): A number that indicates your level. The higher, the better.

• RD (Rating Deviation): The lower, the more reliable your rating. After many matches, RD stabilizes at a low value (~30). If you stop playing, RD increases.

• Volatility (σ): Measures your consistency. If you always finish in similar positions, volatility is low. If you alternate first and last places, volatility rises.

🔥 Why are ALL matches ranked?

At KA ESPORTS, every match you play affects your rating. There are no “friendly” or “practice” matches that don’t count. This is possible because Glicko‑2 automatically adjusts to all skill levels:

BeginnerYour RD is high (350), so your first matches will have a moderate impact until the system fine‑tunes your real level.
Experienced playerYour RD is low (30‑100), so each match will accurately reflect small changes in your performance.
Playing against similarly skilled opponentsPoint distribution is balanced and fair.
There’s an upset (beginner beats master)The system recognizes that something unusual happened and adjusts ratings significantly, but controlled by volatility.

Advantages of this approach:

  • • Every match matters, encouraging constant participation.
  • • The system quickly adapts to your level, without needing separate categories.
  • • It’s impossible to “inflate” your rating by only playing against beginners, because gains will be minimal.
  • • The entire community competes in the same ranking, from novices to legends.

🏁 Race Scoring

Each 4‑player match distributes 96 total points as follows:

PositionRace PointsPlace Points (reference)
1st12🥇 12
2nd8🥈 8
3rd4🥉 4
4th04️⃣ 0

Important: Race points are not the rating. They are the input to the Glicko‑2 algorithm. The rating is calculated after each match by comparing:

• Your result (race points)
• Your opponents’ results
• The rating and RD of all players before the match

This means that finishing first is not enough: if you beat weak opponents, you’ll gain few points; if you finish last but against very strong opponents, you’ll lose few points.

📊 Leaderboards and Reports

• Seasonal leaderboard: Updated monthly. Only players with at least one match in that month appear. Ranks are determined by the matches played in that month.

• Global leaderboard: Includes all active players (last match within ACTIVITY_WINDOW_DAYS). Ranks are based on career totals, but with a higher match requirement for Master/Grand Master (GLOBAL_MIN_MATCHES_FOR_HIGH_RANK).

• Match reports: Detailed view of every processed match, with pre‑ and post‑ratings.

⏳ Inactivity System (Decay)

If a player does not play for a certain period, their RD increases (more uncertainty). Additionally, if their rating is above the decay threshold, they lose a fixed number of points per inactivity period.

🛡️ Anti Smurf and Boosting Detection

The system automatically logs suspicious activities:

  • Excessive rating gain in a short period.
  • Very high win rate with few matches.
  • Low RD with very few matches (possible secondary account).
  • Consecutive matches with the same group of players (boosting).

All alerts are stored in the ANTI_SMURF_LOG sheet for review.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why do I appear as Unranked even though my rating is high?

A: You need to meet the minimum matches requirement. For seasonal, you need at least 5 matches in that month (or 10 if you are in Master/Grand Master). For global, you need at least 5 career matches (or 20 for Master/Grand Master) and have played in the last 30 days.

Q: How is my rating calculated?

A: Using the Glicko‑2 algorithm. It considers your result against each opponent, your RD, and your volatility. The formula is complex but ensures fair adjustments.

Q: What is SOS?

A: Strength of Schedule – the average rating of your opponents before each match. A high SOS means you face tough competition.

Q: Can I lose points even if I finish first?

A: Yes, if your opponents have much lower ratings than you, the expected outcome is a win, so you gain very little – sometimes even a slight loss if the system detects you underperformed relative to expectation.

Q: How often are leaderboards updated?

A: After every match, but the global and seasonal sheets are regenerated after each rebuild. You can manually refresh them via the menu.
